🧘 Stretching Routine

Do this before EVERY session.

1. Cat-Cow

10 reps
  • Start on hands and knees, wrists under shoulders, knees under hips
  • Cow: Drop belly, lift chest and tailbone, look up
  • Cat: Round spine up, tuck chin and tailbone
  • Move slowly with your breath — inhale cow, exhale cat
▶ Watch Demo

2. Thoracic Spine Mobilization

6 reps per side
  • Lie on your side, knees stacked and bent 90°
  • Top arm reaches forward, then rotate open — sweep arm over to opposite side
  • Follow your hand with your eyes, keep knees together
  • Feel the stretch through your mid-back and chest
▶ Watch Demo

3. 90-90 Hip Stretch

6 reps per side
  • Sit on floor with front leg bent 90° in front, back leg bent 90° behind
  • Keep both knees at 90° angles
  • Sit tall, lean torso forward over front shin
  • Rotate to switch sides — both legs transition together
▶ Watch Demo

4. Kneeling Hip Flexor Stretch

6 reps per side
  • Half-kneel position — front foot flat, back knee on ground
  • Squeeze back glute, shift hips forward gently
  • Keep torso tall — don't arch your lower back
  • Feel stretch in the front of the back hip/thigh
▶ Watch Demo

5. Iron Cross Stretch

6 reps per side
  • Lie on back, arms out to sides in a T
  • Lift one leg straight up, then cross it over to opposite hand
  • Keep both shoulders pinned to the ground
  • Feel stretch in hamstrings, glutes, and lower back
▶ Watch Demo

6. Adductor Stretch

6 reps per side
  • Wide stance, shift weight to one side
  • Bend one knee, keep opposite leg straight
  • Feel stretch along inner thigh of the straight leg
  • Keep chest up, hips back
▶ Watch Demo

7. Toe Touches

10 reps
  • Stand with feet together
  • Slowly hinge forward, reaching toward toes
  • Slight bend in knees is OK
  • Roll back up one vertebra at a time
▶ Watch Demo

8. Calf Stretch

5 reps per side
  • Face a wall, step one foot back
  • Keep back heel on floor, lean into wall
  • Feel stretch in back calf
  • Hold 15-20 seconds per rep

🏋️ Strength A

Wk 1-2: Empty pack  |  Wk 3-4: ~10 lbs  |  Wk 5-6: ~20 lbs

1. Front Step-ups

3 sets × 10 reps per leg
  • Face a 12-16" step or bench, wearing your pack
  • Step up with one foot, drive through the heel to stand tall
  • Bring trailing leg up (don't push off back foot)
  • Step down controlled — same leg leads all 10 reps, then switch
▶ Watch Demo

2. Lateral Lunge

3 sets × 10 reps per leg
  • Stand with pack on, feet together
  • Take a wide step to the side, sit hips back and down
  • Keep stepping-side knee tracking over toes, opposite leg straight
  • Push off to return to standing — all reps one side, then switch
▶ Watch Demo

3. Lateral Step-Down Touch

2 sets × 15 reps per leg
Key eccentric exercise for downhill hiking! This trains your quads to control descent.
  • Stand on a step/bench sideways, one foot on the edge
  • Slowly lower the free foot toward the ground — 3 sec descent
  • Lightly tap heel to floor, then drive back up
  • Keep hips level — don't let the standing hip drop
▶ Watch Demo

4. Unilateral Farmer's Carry

2 sets per side
Wk 1-2: 30 sec/side  |  Wk 3-4: 60 sec/side  |  Wk 5-6: 90 sec/side
  • Hold 20-30 lb weight in ONE hand (dumbbell, kettlebell, or jug)
  • Walk with tall posture — fight the lean!
  • Keep shoulders level, core braced
  • Switch hands after the timed carry
▶ Watch Demo

5. Single-Leg Calf Raises

2 sets × 15 reps per leg
  • Stand on one foot on a step edge (heel hanging off)
  • Rise up on toes as high as possible
  • Lower slowly below the step level for full range
  • Hold a wall for balance if needed — pack adds load
▶ Watch Demo

6. Supine Bridge Walk-out

2 sets × timed hold
Wk 1-2: 30 sec  |  Wk 3-4: 45 sec  |  Wk 5-6: 60 sec
  • Lie on back, knees bent, feet flat on floor
  • Bridge up — hips high, squeeze glutes
  • Slowly walk feet OUT away from your body
  • Walk them back in — maintain the bridge the entire time
▶ Watch Demo

7. Front Plank

3 sets × timed hold
Wk 1-2: 30 sec  |  Wk 3-4: 45 sec  |  Wk 5-6: 60 sec
  • Forearms and toes on ground, body straight as a board
  • Elbows under shoulders, squeeze glutes and abs
  • Don't let hips sag or pike up
  • Breathe normally — don't hold your breath!
▶ Watch Demo

8. Prone Cobra

3 sets × timed hold
Wk 1-2: 30 sec  |  Wk 3-4: 45 sec  |  Wk 5-6: 60 sec
  • Lie face down, arms at your sides
  • Lift chest and arms off the floor — squeeze shoulder blades together
  • Palms face down, thumbs rotate out
  • Keep feet on ground, hold position

🚶 Aerobic A — Brisk Walk

Steady-State Endurance Walk

Wk 1-2: 30 min  |  Wk 3-4: 45 min  |  Wk 5-6: 60 min
  • Wear your backpack + hiking shoes
  • Maintain consistent movement — no stopping
  • Pace: fast enough to elevate HR & breathing, not so fast you must stop
  • Conversational pace — you can talk but it's an effort

🪜 Aerobic B — Stair Intervals

10 × 60-Second Stair Intervals

Wk 1-2: Empty pack  |  Wk 3-4: ~10 lbs  |  Wk 5-6: ~20 lbs
  • Find stairs covering 2-3 floors
  • Wear backpack + hiking shoes
  • Go up and down at a rapid, consistent pace for 60 seconds
  • Rest 2 minutes
  • Repeat × 10 rounds
CARRY your pack in your arms instead of wearing it — builds upper body strength for loading/unloading gear at camp!

🥾 Aerobic C — Distance Hike

Young Man Trail, South Mountain

Wk 1-2: 4 miles  |  Wk 3-4: 6 miles  |  Wk 5-6: 8 miles
  • Wear backpack + hiking shoes, pack at trip weight
  • Emphasis on DISTANCE — speed doesn't matter
  • Stops are OK — just complete the full mileage
  • Trail = 2 mi / 894 ft gain per loop
  • Wk 1-2: 2 loops | Wk 3-4: 3 loops | Wk 5-6: 4 loops

⭐ Bonus Exercises

Add these to Strength A and B for extra preparation. These target the exact demands of canyon hiking.

Bodyweight Pistol Squat Progressions

3 sets × 5-8 reps per leg

Add to Strength A days

  • Start with: Single-leg sit-to-stand from a chair or bench
  • Sit down on one leg, other leg extended forward
  • Stand back up without using hands
  • Progress to: Lower bench/box → eventually no bench (full pistol)
  • This is the ultimate downhill hiking exercise — pure eccentric single-leg control
▶ Watch Demo

Bulgarian Split Squat

3 sets × 8-10 reps per leg

Add to Strength B days

  • Rear foot elevated on a bench or step behind you
  • Lower into a deep lunge — front knee tracks over toes
  • Drive through front heel to stand back up
  • Wear your pack for added load
  • Builds massive single-leg strength for trail switchbacks
▶ Watch Demo

Romanian Deadlift (RDL)

3 sets × 10-12 reps

Add to Strength A or B days

  • Hold dumbbells or kettlebell in front of thighs
  • Slight knee bend, hinge at hips — push butt BACK
  • Lower weights along your legs until you feel hamstring stretch
  • Squeeze glutes to stand back up — don't round your back!
  • Builds posterior chain for uphill climbs (Bright Angel out)
▶ Watch Demo

Single-Leg Romanian Deadlift

2 sets × 8 reps per leg

Add to Strength B days (after RDL is comfortable)

  • Stand on one leg, slight knee bend
  • Hinge forward, extending free leg behind you
  • Lower until torso is near parallel to floor
  • Squeeze glute to return — hold a dumbbell for load
  • Builds balance + single-leg strength for uneven terrain

📋 YMCA Step Test

Test Dates: Mar 31 · Apr 20 · May 11

Equipment: 12-inch step, stopwatch, metronome at 96 BPM

  1. Set metronome to 96 BPM
  2. Step up/down following the beat: up, up, down, down
  3. Continue for exactly 3 minutes
  4. Immediately sit down
  5. Find pulse (wrist or neck)
  6. Count beats for 60 seconds

🏆 Excellent< 76 bpm
✅ Good80-88 bpm
👍 Above Average92-98 bpm
➡️ Average100-105 bpm

Target: Above Average or higher after completing the program.
